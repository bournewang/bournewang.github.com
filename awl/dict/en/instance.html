/ˈɪnstəns/
noun
1. Urgency of manner or words; an urgent request; insistence.
2. A token; a sign; a symptom or indication.
It sends some precious instance of itself/ After the thing it loves. Hamlet IV. v. ca. 1602
3. That which is urgent; motive.
4. Occasion; order of occurrence.
5. A case offered as an exemplification or a precedent; an illustrative example.
6. One of a series of recurring occasions, cases, essentially the same.
7. A piece of evidence; a proof or sign (of something).
8. A specific occurrence of something that is created or instantiated, such as a database, or an object of a class in object-oriented programming.
9. (massively multiplayer online games) A dungeon or other area that is duplicated for each player, or each party of players, that enters it, so that each player or party has a private copy of the area, isolated from other players.
10. (massively multiplayer online games) An individual copy of such a dungeon or other area.
verb
1. To mention as a case or example; to refer to; to cite
2. To cite an example as proof; to exemplify.
