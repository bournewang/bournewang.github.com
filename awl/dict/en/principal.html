[/ˈpɹɪnsəpəl/][/ˈpɹɪnsəp̬əl/]
noun
1. The money originally invested or loaned, on which basis interest and returns are calculated.
2. The chief administrator of a school.
3. The chief executive and chief academic officer of a university or college.
4. A legal person that authorizes another (the agent) to act on their behalf; or on whose behalf an agent or gestor in a negotiorum gestio acts.
5. The primary participant in a crime.
6. A partner or owner of a business.
7. A diapason, a type of organ stop on a pipe organ.
8. The construction that gives shape and strength to a roof, generally a truss of timber or iron; or, loosely, the most important member of a piece of framing.
9. The first two long feathers of a hawk's wing.
10. One of the turrets or pinnacles of waxwork and tapers with which the posts and centre of a funeral hearse were formerly crowned.
11. An essential point or rule; a principle.
12. A dancer at the highest rank within a professional dance company, particularly a ballet company.
13. A security principal.
synonyms: dean, client, diapason, proprietor, ringleader, headmaster, headmistress, headteacher
adjective
1. Primary; most important; first level in importance.
2. (Latinism) Of or relating to a prince; princely.
synonyms: chief, main, primary
